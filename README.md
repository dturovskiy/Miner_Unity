# Scenario

The essence of the game: find a valuable nugget consisting of four blocks located underground on the map.
Map size: 250 x 100 tiles. Each tile is 128 pixels.

The game has two scenes:

1. MenuScene
2. MainScene — where the entire gameplay takes place

## MenuScene

The start scene, which has buttons:

* Exit
* Settings
* Start Game

## MainScene

* The main scene where all the gameplay happens
* GameController — an object that controls game loading and main buttons

The scene includes buttons:

* Menu
* Map
* Joystick
* UI elements

## Concept

This type of game is quite popular among mobile phone users on the Android platform. The player controls a miner. The game begins with the miner following signs and entering the mine to extract coal. However, as the miner goes deeper into the mine, it turns out that there are not only coal but also metals, precious stones, other minerals, and artifacts. In addition, there are obstacles such as rocks. If a rock falls on the miner’s head or he jumps from a great height, he dies.

The player can adjust music and sound effects volume. There is a choice of language and control modes. Vibration can be toggled; when turned off, a red cross appears. There are 4 control modes. The game should be saved periodically: in case you lose or your phone battery runs out.

## Rules

"Little Miner": In-game store purchases

From the very start of the game, you have a tent for health recovery, a store, and a bank. The store is meant for buying inventory items. At the start, you have 20 steps, 20 supports, 20 signs, 20 bridges, and 3 medkits. Let’s review them:

1. **Step:** allows climbing one level up.
2. **Support:** needed to prevent rocks from falling on the head (steps can also be used for the same purpose).
3. **Bridge:** serves to build a path between two tracks to avoid falling down.
4. **Map:** reveals part of the mine; you can see what minerals are nearby (sometimes you can find a map among deposits).
5. **Medkit:** restores health.
6. **Signs:** help better navigate the tunnels.

You can also upgrade essential tools in the store:

1. **Pickaxe:** upgrades allow digging faster.
2. **Cart:** after each upgrade, it can carry more units of ore.
3. **Lamp:** shines brighter with each upgrade.

During the game, you not only accumulate money but also upgrade your inventory. The pickaxe, cart, and lamp have a limited number of upgrades.

At the bank, you can earn game money by completing various tasks: watching suggested videos, installing apps from the Play Market on your smartphone.

The game starts with the miner standing near the tent, store, and bank. The tent is not needed for now. Check what the bank wants from you. Watch a few videos and earn some money. Then head to the mine. Before each mine visit, check how many steps, supports, and bridges you have. It’s good to have several medkits, but you can manage without them in the first few minutes. You work underground with a pickaxe. Try to dig out an area with minerals. Initially, your cart holds 10 units of mined minerals — coal first, then iron. At deeper layers, gold and artifacts appear. Try to collect artifacts; they pay well. As the game progresses, you won’t be interested in bank tasks much because minerals pay more. Once the cart is full, climb up and return. Avoid falling into pits and rock collapses; otherwise, you lose and must start over or from the last saved point. Therefore, save the game periodically.

When you first collect a certain type of mineral, you will be informed of its value. So pay attention. The deeper the mine, the more valuable the prizes. If coal earns you a few dollars, some ores can bring from 100 to 500 dollars. This means for 10 units of coal and iron, you can earn 70 or 80 dollars. For emeralds, uranium, and platinum, your income can be around 2000 bucks. So plan your route to iron ores carefully. Try to avoid areas with many rocks. During the game, you may find maps that reveal parts of the mine. You can also open new mine sections using your maps. This way, you will always see where to go.

The upgrade strategy is as follows. First, upgrade the pickaxe — so you can dig properly and quickly. Next, upgrade the cart: you will be able to mine more minerals. Then make a couple of lamp upgrades. This is one strategy. But you can buy tools in any order you want. The only downside of the game is that it’s hard to stop playing. The game ends when you find the big nugget. After that, the game is considered completed.
